<Warning>
  All exit plans account for a family with **2 dogs**. Routes that cannot accommodate pets are noted. Indonesian passport holders have visa-free access to Malaysia (90 days), Singapore (30 days), and most ASEAN countries.
</Warning>

## Route 1: Direct Flight Out ✈️

**Priority: 1 (when available)**

Fly from DXB or DWC to Jakarta (CGK), Bali (DPS), Kuala Lumpur (KUL), Singapore (SIN), Mumbai (BOM), or Colombo (CMB).

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Details">
    - Check DXB/DWC operational status FIRST
    - Pet transport requires advance booking (Emirates, Etihad have pet cargo)
    - If commercial flights suspended, check charter operators at Al Maktoum (DWC)
    - If Gulf airspace compromised, routes may divert south over Oman/Arabian Sea
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Pet-Friendly Airlines">
    - **Emirates SkyCargo** — pet cargo available, verify crisis availability
    - **Etihad Cargo** — pet transport, call ahead
    - **Singapore Airlines** — pet cargo on select routes
    - **Oman Air Cargo** — from Muscat (MCT) if using Route 2
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Route 2: Drive to Oman (Muscat) 🚗

**Priority: 2 — BEST OPTION when airports are closed**

Drive from Dubai to Muscat (~4 hours). From Muscat, fly to KUL/SIN/CGK.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose Border Crossing">
    - **Hatta crossing:** Dubai → Hatta → Oman border (~1.5 hrs to border)
    - **Al Ain crossing:** Dubai → Al Ain → Al Buraimi → Muscat (~4 hrs total)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Entry Requirements">
    - Indonesian passport holders: visa on arrival normally available for Oman
    - **Dogs:** Oman requires rabies vaccination certificate — have documents ready
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare for Drive">
    - Fuel up FULL before leaving
    - Carry extra water and food supplies
    - Avoid driving at night during active conflict
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fly from Muscat (MCT)">
    - Muscat airport may have more capacity than UAE airports
    - Target flights: Oman Air to KUL, DEL, HYD, then connect to Jakarta
    - Book pet cargo through Oman Air ahead of time
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Route 3: Sea via Fujairah 🚢

**Priority: 3 — Last resort before land**

Travel to Fujairah port (~1.5 hrs from Dubai). Fujairah is on the Gulf of Oman — **OUTSIDE the Strait of Hormuz chokepoint**.

- This is a slow, uncertain option
- Dogs travel more easily on private/chartered vessels
- Contact Indonesian embassy for coordinated naval evacuation if available
- Bring life jackets, water, food for 48+ hours

## Route 4: Drive South (Extended) 🚗

**Priority: 4 — Desperation only**

Drive south through Al Ain toward Oman interior or west toward Saudi Arabia.

- Very long drive (10+ hours to Salalah, Oman)
- Saudi Arabia requires visa for Indonesian passport holders — may not be viable unless border opened for refugees
- Carry significant fuel, water, and food
- From Salalah: flights or sea routes to India/Sri Lanka possible
