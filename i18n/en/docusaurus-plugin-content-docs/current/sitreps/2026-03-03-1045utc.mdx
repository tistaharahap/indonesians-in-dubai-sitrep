1. **TAKE ANY AVAILABLE FLIGHT OUT** — Contact Emirates, flydubai, Air Arabia NOW. Prioritize flights to Indonesia or safe transits (India, Singapore, Malaysia). The window narrows every hour.
2. **CONTACT KJRI Dubai & KBRI Abu Dhabi** — Register for evacuation if you haven't. Request information on repatriation flights.
3. **GO-BAG AT THE DOOR** — Passport, cash (Dirhams + USD), medications, water, shelf-stable food, phone chargers, copies of critical documents.
4. **AVOID STRATEGIC AREAS** — Stay away from military airports, ports, oil facilities, or military bases.
5. **PREPARE LAND ROUTE** — If no flights available: Dubai → Al Ain → Oman (if safe) → sea evacuation from Muscat. OR Dubai → Abu Dhabi → KBRI for coordination.

### Households with Children

- **Highest priority for air evacuation** — Children are most vulnerable during prolonged emergencies
- Prepare entertainment, sufficient food/milk for 72 hours, children's medications
- Identify nearest shelters (building basements, underground malls)

### Households with Pets

- **Vaccination and animal documents MUST be carried** — Without these, animals are denied entry to most countries
- Prepare carriers, pet food for 72 hours
- **CRITICAL**: Many evacuation flights do NOT accept animals. Land route to Oman + flight from Muscat MAY be more realistic if Oman still accepts passengers
- **LAST RESORT**: If urgent air evacuation and pets cannot come, board with an acquaintance who is STAYING or with a pet boarding facility. This is a LAST-RESORT option only if life is threatened

### Households with Mobility/Medical Constraints

- Contact KBRI/KJRI for special evacuation assistance
- Ensure minimum 2-week medication supply
- Identify nearest hospital and fastest route there
- If self-evacuation is not possible, STAY INDOORS away from windows and register special needs with the embassy

The following is analytical reasoning based on available information at the time of writing. It is not fact, not conclusive, and represents one possible interpretation. All posture claims are grounded in the factual developments reported above.

- **Iran strikes Fujairah (UAE's alternative oil route)**: This may signal Iran's refusal to allow the UAE a "safe zone" outside Hormuz. Message to UAE: neutrality is not enough, as long as US bases operate from your territory.
- **Iran strikes Oman (Salalah/Raysut + Duqm)**: Oman has been the Iran-US peace broker for years. These strikes may signal Iran views all Gulf states permitting US infrastructure as legitimate targets, or that Iran is losing operational control over its broader target set.
- **Saudi "strongly condemns" + threatens retaliation**: Saudi Arabia shifts from silent to confrontational. If Saudi enters militarily, this significantly widens the war — Saudi has one of the world's largest defense budgets.
- **US closes 4 embassies + orders 14 countries "leave now"**: This is a US assessment that civilian security in the Gulf region **can no longer be guaranteed**. This is the strongest signal the US expects further escalation.
- **France offers nuclear jets + Iran warns EU**: Escalation to a European nuclear dimension signals this conflict could extend far beyond the Middle East. Iran likely seeks to deter EU involvement, while France may intend solidarity without actual nuclear use.
